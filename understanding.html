What is Mental Health?

Mental health includes our emotional, psychological, and social well-being. It affects how we think, feel, and act. Good mental health allows us to handle stress, relate to others, and make healthy decisions.

Common Mental Health Conditions

Understanding mental health conditions can help reduce stigma and encourage early treatment.

  • Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder (OCD): A condition where individuals experience persistent, unwanted thoughts and engage in repetitive behaviors.
  • Bipolar Disorder: Characterized by extreme mood swings, including emotional highs (mania) and lows (depression).
  • Schizophrenia: A severe mental disorder affecting thoughts, feelings, and behaviors, often causing hallucinations or delusions.
  • Personality Disorders: Patterns of behavior, cognition, and inner experience that deviate from cultural expectations, causing distress or difficulty in functioning.

Signs of Mental Health Issues

Recognizing the signs early can help in seeking timely intervention.

  • Persistent feelings of sadness or hopelessness
  • Extreme mood changes
  • Withdrawal from social activities
  • Changes in sleeping or eating patterns
  • Increased substance use

How to Improve Mental Well-being

  1. Engage in regular physical activity.
  2. Practice mindfulness and stress management techniques.
  3. Maintain a healthy diet and sleep routine.
  4. Stay connected with family and friends.
  5. Seek professional help when needed.
